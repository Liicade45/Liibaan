Somali National University (SNU)

Established: 1954 (Official university status in 1969)

Location: Mogadishu, Somalia (Main campus: Jaamacada Gaheyr)

Overview

The Somali National University (SNU), known in Somali as Jaamacadda Ummadda Soomaaliyeed, is a leading public institution of higher education in Somalia. Originally established during the Italian trusteeship period, it has grown into a major university serving students from across the country.

Mission and Vision

SNU aims to offer affordable and equitable world-class education. The university is committed to producing skilled professionals who can help solve the country’s socio-economic and technological challenges.

Faculties and Programs

SNU offers a range of undergraduate programs through various faculties, including:

  • Faculty of Agriculture and Environmental Science
  • Faculty of Veterinary Medicine and Animal Husbandry
  • Faculty of Sharia and Islamic Studies

Admissions

Applicants must:

  • Complete the official application form
  • Pay a non-refundable application fee
  • Submit certified academic certificates and transcripts
  • Provide a certified National ID or birth certificate

Notable Alumni

SNU has educated many influential leaders, including ministers and members of parliament, making it a cornerstone in shaping Somalia’s future leadership.

For more details, visit the official website: snu.edu.so
